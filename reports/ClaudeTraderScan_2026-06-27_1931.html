What the signals mean (plain English)

The indicator numbers shown under each pick

Number What it is How to read it
RSI (0–100) Relative Strength Index over 14 days — a momentum gauge. Below 30 = oversold (beaten down, may bounce); 30–70 = normal; above 70 = overbought (crowded, often due a pullback).
MACD 12-day EMA minus 26-day EMA — trend direction. Above 0 = up-trend momentum, below 0 = down-trend. The further from 0, the stronger the trend.
MACD hist MACD line minus its 9-day signal line — momentum change. Positive & rising = momentum building; a flip from − to + is an early bullish trigger; negative & falling = weakening.
Risk %/day Average daily price swing (ATR as a % of price) — volatility. Under 2% = calm; 2–4% = active; over 4% = high-risk. Higher volatility means bigger potential gains and bigger drawdowns — it cuts both ways.
